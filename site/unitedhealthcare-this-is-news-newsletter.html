UnitedHealthcare

United Health Care Design, Development & QA

This project demonstrates my ability to take a basic Word document brief with brand assets and transform it into a fully designed and then coded email. I handled the process end-to-end: interpreting the brief, applying design principles, and developing a responsive, cross-client-compatible HTML email.

Interpreting the Brief

Extracted key messaging and visual hierarchy from the Word document.

Identified essential content sections like the headline, callouts (e.g., Service Heroes), and closing remarks for emphasis.

Design Stage

Visual Layout: Created a clear and engaging layout based on the content, emphasizing headers, quotes, and recognition sections.

Brand Consistency: Applied UnitedHealthcare's brand guidelines, including typography, colors, and image placement.

Mobile-First Approach: Designed with responsive layouts to ensure readability and functionality across devices.

Development Stage

HTML Coding: Hand-coded the email to reflect the design, ensuring clean, maintainable, and responsive code.

Cross-Client Compatibility: Addressed quirks in email clients like Outlook using techniques such as inline styles and VML for background images.

Accessibility Compliance: Added alt text for images and structured the content for screen readers to ensure usability for all audiences.

Quality Assurance

Rendering Tests: Used Email on Acid to validate design consistency across 90+ email clients.

Functionality Tests: Verified all links and interactive elements worked correctly.

Performance Optimization: Minimized load time by compressing assets and cleaning up code.
